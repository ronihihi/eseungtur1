  <div class="section">
    <div class="section-title"><span class="icon">📐</span> Design Principles for Web</div>
    <div class="principles">
      <div class="principle-card">
        <h4>Flexibility</h4>
        <p>No single "right way" to combine elements. Experiment, innovate — just keep each element recognizable.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="principle-card">
        <h4>Anchoring</h4>
        <p>Anchor design elements and images to the edge of sections or where background color blocks meet. Creates stability.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="principle-card" style="border-top-color:var(--red);">
        <h4>Depth</h4>
        <p>Layer elements: white background → photo → speech bubble → line → color block → logo. Use soft outer glow (box-shadow) on bordered shapes.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="principle-card">
        <h4>Curvature</h4>
        <p>Use rounded corners (border-radius) to balance sharp edges. The brand mixes bold rectangles with rounded cards and the iconic bubble shape.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="principle-card" style="border-top-color:var(--red);">
        <h4>Asymmetry</h4>
        <p>Use asymmetric margins. Elements like the title line, logo, or bubble can intentionally cross into the margin — this is a brand feature, not a bug.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
